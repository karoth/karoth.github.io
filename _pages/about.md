Welcome! I am Kim, a mathematician and statistician at [Juniata College](https://www.juniata.edu). Originally my training is in pure mathematics, complex dynamical systems to be precise, but have since trained in statistics where I do statistical consulting for microbial genomics in the Lamendella Lab. My interests also include the Scholarship of Teaching and Learning both in mathematics and statistics. I am part of both the [mathematics department](https://www.juniata.edu/academics/departments/mathematics/) and the [data science program](https://www.juniata.edu/academics/departments/mathematics/) including the newly launched [data science masters.](https://www.juniata.edu/academics/graduate-programs/data-science.php). 

My Ph.D is in [mathematics](https://math.psu.edu) from The Pennsylvania State University and my [Masters in Applied Statistics](https://www.worldcampus.psu.edu/degrees-and-certificates/applied-statistics-masters/overview) is from the Pennsylvania State University's World Campus. My bachelor's degree is in [mathematics](https://www.oberlin.edu/arts-and-sciences/departments/mathematics) with a minor in [computer science](https://www.oberlin.edu/arts-and-sciences/departments/computer-science) from Oberlin College.
